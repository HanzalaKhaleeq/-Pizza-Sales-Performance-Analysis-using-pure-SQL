# Pizza Sales Performance Analysis (SQL Case Study)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project features a comprehensive end-to-end SQL analysis of a fictional pizza restaurant's sales data. Processing over **21,000+ orders** and **$817.8K in total revenue**, the objective was to transition raw transactional data into actionable operational and financial insights.

The analysis answers 8 core business questions ranging from basic data retrieval to advanced metrics like percentage revenue contribution, utilizing complex aggregations, multi-table joins, and nested subqueries.

---

# 📊 Key Business Insights
**Operational Peak:** A distinct lunch-hour rush occurs at **12:00 PM** (generating 2,520 orders), pinpointing optimal staffing and inventory windows.
*   **Volume Drivers:** The **Classic** pizza category and **Large (L)** size dominate customer preference by a wide margin.
*   **Revenue Distribution:** Revenue is healthy and evenly distributed across the menu. **The Thai Chicken Pizza** stands as the top financial contributor, accounting for **5.31%** ($43,434.25) of total sales.

## 💡 Business Questions Solved
1. **Total Revenue:** Calculated total revenue generated from sales ($817,860.05).
2. **Highest-Priced Menu Item:** Identified the top-tier item (The Greek Pizza at $35.95) using subqueries and ordering boundaries.
3. **Common Size Choice:** Extracted the most frequent size ordered (Large with 18,526 orders).
4. **Top 5 Bestsellers:** Isolated the highest-volume pizza types by quantities sold.
5. **Category Distribution:** Broke down total quantities ordered across all main pizza categories.
6. **Hourly Traffic:** Modeled order distribution by the hour to locate peak operational traffic.
7. **Top 3 Revenue Generators:** Ranked items by gross income to locate core revenue anchors.
8. **Percentage Revenue Contribution:** Evaluated individual pizza types against total accumulated revenue to check menu health.
